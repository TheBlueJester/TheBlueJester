Java



Java is a German-style boardgame published in 2000. The game plays out on the island of Java on a hexagonal board.
Players build the island and score by setting up palace festivals at opportune moments.
When players run out of hexagons to build the island, the game is over.
A final scoring phase now takes place and a winner is declared.

Memoir'44



Memoir'44 is a war-themed strategy board game for two players published in 2004.
The game also contains rules for alternate modes that allow:
- 2 teams of 3 to play and an,
- "Overlord" scenario for up to eight players requiring 2 copies of the game.
The game simulates over a dozen different battles associated with the D-Day invasions in World War II

Monopoly



Monopoly is a multiplayer economics themed board game.
Players buy, trade and develop properties in order to create an environment that will bankrupt other players.
The game contains randomized elements via Dice Throws and 2 Decks of Card containing various random events and effects
Monopoly has been an international hit being licensed in 103+ countries and printed in more than 37 languages.

Players: 2+
Setup Time: 2 - 5 mins

Scrabble



Scrabble is a word game played by placing tiles onto a grided board.
Tiles contain single letters and players must spell complete words using tiles from their hands and those already in place on the board.
Words placed by players must connect or otherwise build off existing words and tiles on the board in a crossword like fashion.
The game is hugely popular around with world with over 150 million sets having been sold across the globe.

Players: 2 - 4
Setup Time: 4 - 8 mins
Play Time: ~50 mins
Age Range: 8+

Shogun



Shogun, also known by the name Samurai Swords, is a game about fictional Warlords set in Feudal Japan.
Players take on the role of these Warlords and control a number of military assets including Generals and their armies.
The goal of the game is to strategically leverage your assets in order to take territories and ultimately claim the title of Shogun.

Ticket to Ride



Ticket to Ride is a railway themed boardgame published in 2004.
The game takes place on a map of a railway system of the United States and Canada.
However many variations now exist based on railway systems from around the world.
Players compete to score the most points by completing randomized goals from a deck of cards.
Each card has a different city pair to connect via railway and grants a different number of points for completing the task.

Tikal



Tikal is an adventure themed games about exploring the jungles of Central America.
Players send teams of explorers into the jungle in order to expose new areas and search for Temples.
Players compete to earn the most points by finding artifacts, excavating and maintaining control over discovered temples.
